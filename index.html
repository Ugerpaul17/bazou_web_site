About Our Charity

The idea of forming a non-profit organization was born in April 2015 when I, “Mako” was selected as the Minister of Women Affairs in the Kingdom of Bazou, by the King, His Majesty, Vincent Kemajou Tchoua and Notables of Bazou Kingdom, Cameroon, in addition to holding the title of the Queen and Mother of the King and Kingdom of Bazou. The adoption of the slogan “Educate a Woman, Educate a Village” reemphasizes our belief that if we empower rural women in Africa with the knowledge and capabilities of utilizing their energy and available natural resources, we can create sustainable communities, since women are the basis of many African families and play important leadership roles.

We are blessed and very fortunate that we are surrounded by and are working with many individuals who share the same goals and collectively we can make a difference in improving the lives of these undeserved groups of people. In August 2021, after the effect of the corona virus pandemic hit its toll in the world, a group of experts and I decided to officially take the “dream” to a higher level by creating and implementing an international non-profit organization “Mako Royale Bazou” registered and incorporated in the State of California as a 501(c) (3) corporation whose purpose is to empower rural women in Africa and create sustainable communities.

Our Mission

The mission of Mako Royale Bazou (MRB), a non-profit organization, is focused on empowering rural women in Africa by creating institutions that promote the utilization of energy and available natural resources and/or working in synergy with existing institutions with similar goals.

Our Vision

The vision of MRB is geared towards the creation of sustainable communities in the rural communities of Africa. These areas are mostly savannah grasslands which have a potential of offering ample opportunities in crop and animal husbandry. It is important to understand that women are the basis of many rural families and play important leadership roles: by empowering women, the organization would create stability in rural communities and thereby mitigating the influx of population to cities where the unemployed resort to various undesirable activities and crime such as and not limited to loitering, prostitution, vandalism, and petty theft.
